# Idea Analysis

### English

#### 1. Problem Statement

- This idea aims to create an online Bầu Cua Tôm Cá game where people can play together in a shared, rule-based room experience.
- It solves the need for a social, repeatable game that supports daily engagement, friendly competition, and room-based play with clear entry conditions.
- The problem matters because players need a simple way to join matches, keep progressing through points, and have reasons to return every day.

#### 2. Target Users

- Primary users are people who want to play Bầu Cua Tôm Cá online with other users.
- Primary users also include people who want to host a room and manage who can join.
- Secondary users are players who participate for daily rewards, missions, and casual entertainment.
- Indirect users may include community members who invite friends to join a room-based game experience.

#### 3. Value Proposition

- The idea delivers a familiar game experience in an online format with progression, daily rewards, and room-based social play.
- It improves the user’s situation by giving them a clear reason to return every day, a sense of progress through points, and a way to play with friends or other players.
- It also gives room hosts control over access and room visibility, which helps shape different play experiences.

#### 4. In-Scope

- A traditional Bầu Cua Tôm Cá game experience.
- User login and point-based play.
- Starting users with 1000 points.
- No point top-up; points are earned only through in-game events and rewards.
- Daily login reward of 200 points.
- Daily missions:
  - Play 5 rounds to receive 200 points.
  - Win 3 rounds to receive 300 points.
  - Place a total of 5000 points in bets to receive 500 points.
- Room creation by a host.
- Public and private room visibility chosen by the host.
- Minimum point requirement for entering and staying in a room.
- Automatic removal of users who fall below the required minimum while inside the room.
- Room joining allowed when a user meets the table’s minimum bet requirement.

#### 5. Out-of-Scope / Non-Goals

- Point purchases or any form of recharge.
- Administrative or moderation features for now.
- Changing the core traditional Bầu Cua rules.
- Expanding into broader casino or unrelated game features.
- Complex social features beyond room-based multiplayer play.

#### 6. Assumptions

- Players understand the traditional Bầu Cua rules or can learn them quickly.
- Daily rewards and missions are enough to encourage repeat play.
- A point-only economy will be motivating even without top-ups.
- Room-based play is sufficient for the intended social experience.
- The minimum point rules will feel fair and understandable to players.
- The game experience will remain enjoyable even with strict room entry and retention conditions.

#### 7. Risks & Unknowns

- The game may face legal, policy, or perception risks because it resembles gambling even though it uses points only.
- The balance of rewards and betting limits may need careful tuning to avoid making progression too slow or too easy.
- The minimum point rule may frustrate players if they are removed from rooms too often.
- Public/private room behavior may need further clarification for player expectations.
- The analysis does not yet define how players discover available rooms, such as browsing, filtering, search, or suggestions.
- The relationship between the room's minimum bet requirement and the room's minimum point requirement still needs to be confirmed.
- Session length and round pacing are still open questions, which may affect retention and reward timing.
- The target market and localization scope have not yet been defined, so regional expectations remain unclear.
- The reward structure may become repetitive if there are not enough reasons to keep playing beyond daily missions.
- Open questions remain about session length, how players discover rooms, and how competitive or casual the experience should feel.
